## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Church Profile

**Size:** 50-200 members (sweet spot: 75-150)

**Characteristics:**
- Traditional/evangelical denomination (Baptist, Presbyterian, Methodist, Non-denominational)
- Single location (not multi-site)
- Located in suburban or rural areas
- Budget: $50,000-$200,000/year
- Tech-savvy: Low to medium (using email, maybe Facebook)
- Existing tools: Microsoft Word for bulletins, Google Calendar, email lists

**Decision Makers:**
- Pastor (final say, budget approval)
- Church secretary/administrator (day-to-day user, main pain point)
- Elder board (approves spending >$500)

### Pain Points

**Primary (Secretary/Admin):**
1. **Drowning in bulletin chaos**
   - Creates bulletin from scratch every week in Word
   - Last-minute changes from pastor Friday afternoon
   - Typos slip through, formatting breaks
   - Takes 3-5 hours per week

2. **Announcement overload**
   - 15+ requests per week, hard to track
   - Email, text, phone calls, after-service hallway asks
   - No central place to collect them
   - Feels like a gatekeeper (saying no is hard)

3. **Event coordination nightmare**
   - Events in multiple places (paper calendar, Google Cal, emails)
   - RSVPs via text, email, phone - no single list
   - Can't easily see who's coming

4. **Time stress**
   - Works 10-15 hours/week (part-time)
   - Unpaid overtime every week
   - Burnout risk high

**Secondary (Pastor):**
- Wants better engagement tracking (who's reading announcements?)
- Wishes bulletin looked more professional
- Frustrated by last-minute "can you add this?" culture

**Tertiary (Members):**
- Elderly members struggle with small print in bulletin
- Miss announcements (not in bulletin or forgot)
- Don't know how to RSVP to events

### Jobs To Be Done

When I use [Elder-First Platform], I want to:
1. **Reduce bulletin creation time** from 3-5 hours to 30 minutes
2. **Eliminate last-minute chaos** with hard Thursday lock deadline
3. **Centralize announcement collection** in one intake form
4. **Produce professional-looking bulletins** without design skills
5. **Track event RSVPs** in one place
6. **Make content accessible** to elderly members (large text, high contrast)

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk:** Market too small (not enough small churches)
**Mitigation:** 180,000 churches in US under 200 members. 1% penetration = 1,800 customers = $1.7M ARR

**Risk:** Competitors (Planning Center) lower price
**Mitigation:** Focus on elder-first UX and bulletin-first approach. They can't easily copy simplicity.

**Risk:** Churches don't want to change
**Mitigation:** 30-day trial with concierge onboarding. Make switching painless.

**Risk:** Low trial-to-paid conversion
**Mitigation:** Onboarding call to get first win fast (first bulletin created). Weekly check-ins during trial.

**Risk:** High churn
**Mitigation:** Customer success team checks in quarterly. Annual discount (17% off) to lock in.
